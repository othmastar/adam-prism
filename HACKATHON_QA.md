## 🎯 General Q&A Tips (Carnegie throughout)

### ✅ Do:
1. **Begin with appreciation** (Carnegie #1, #2) — even of competitors
2. **Be a good listener** (Carnegie #7) — let them finish
3. **Talk in their interest** (Carnegie #8) — their cost, their risk, their sovereignty
4. **Admit limitations** (Carnegie #12) — builds trust
5. **Sympathize** (Carnegie #17) — with their burden
6. **Appeal to nobler motives** (Carnegie #18) — "you protect millions"
7. **Throw down a challenge** (Carnegie #20) — "are you ready?"
8. **End with "yes"** (Carnegie #14) — get agreement

### ❌ Don't:
1. **Don't criticize competitors** (Carnegie #1) — even gently
2. **Don't say "you're wrong"** (Carnegie #21) — be indirect
3. **Don't oversell tech** — judges want ROI
4. **Don't promise AI AGI** — focus on sovereignty
5. **Don't be defensive** — judges will challenge, embrace it
6. **Don't argue** (Carnegie #10) — agree and redirect
